## who

Jon Ninas — channel **Ninjon** — is a recognised miniature-painting expert (Warhammer / tabletop
hobby) with a decade-plus of YouTube IP and a loyal, trust-driven audience. His authority comes from
years of demonstrated skill and the community's trust in his integrity; that personal integrity is
treated as the core asset to protect in everything produced in his name.

## voice

Warm, friendly, expert-but-approachable — the "Jon on your shoulder" feel (his greeting register is
literally "Hey again, friends"). Recommendations and any article direction should sound like Jon
helping a friend at the painting desk, never auto-generated or generic. Avoid the tell-tale
AI-content repetition (e.g. repeated stock greetings). Hand-holding without dumbing down: give value
at every level, don't dump everything before the learner knows the first thing.

## audience

Miniature/tabletop painters across the full range — total beginners through experienced hobbyists.
Trust-driven and **anti-AI-art-sensitive**: this community reacts badly to anything that smells of
AI-generated art. They want practical, demonstrable skill help and product guidance (it's a
tool-and-purchase-heavy "golf-money" hobby — "what's the X you're using?" is a constant question).
Watch for the beginner-vs-expert split: the same comment section spans both, and the knowledge base
needs guided pathways for each.

## what_they_sell

**Deck of Many Colors** — a physical product, already live and selling (real, licensed artists), the
proven revenue anchor; everything new should feed back toward it. Merch is moving in-house. Longer
term: an owned-audience ecosystem (email + on-site KB + possible community/tools). No paid course
today — the current build is a **website knowledge base** that turns Jon's YouTube videos into
guided-walkthrough articles.

## funnel

**Attention (social/YouTube) → captured into owned audience (email, via Kit) → guided journey on-site
(the knowledge base) → product/community (Deck of Many Colors) → users share progress → new
attention.** The strategic centre of gravity is the **user journey** — what people do once they
arrive: frictionless entry, an obvious next step, a reason to circle back. Insight should connect to
that journey: what article/skill people need next, and what pulls them toward the Deck.

## strategic_phase

Pre-remit pitch + early KB build (2026). The site is moving Squarespace → Shopify; a large archive of
AI-generated articles built from Jon's transcripts exists but is "close but not there" on tone (Jon
asked for real video screen-grabs over filler). What matters now: article direction that is
demonstrably from the audience's real needs, protects Jon's voice, and strengthens the guided journey
toward email capture and the Deck. Parked (don't centre yet): the "Ask Jon" AI bot and the
Wirecutter-style review hub.

## sensitivities

- **AI-assisted, never AI-generated.** Non-negotiable. Never frame articles/insight as AI-generated;
  the artists behind the Deck are real and licensed. Integrity first — this community is anti-AI-art.
- **No rushed output.** Jon wants ~90% confidence before anything reaches the audience ("first
  impression is the only impression"); flag where input is thin rather than over-claiming.
- **Tone fidelity.** Recommendations must preserve Jon's specific friendly-expert voice; flag drift
  toward generic/auto-generated phrasing.
- Beginner and expert share the comment section — separate their needs; don't let expert voices
  dictate beginner-pathway decisions.
